Privacy Policy

Undercover — Last updated: 19 June 2026

Undercover is a local multiplayer word game. This page explains exactly what data is and is not collected when you use the app.

What we collect

The app uses Cloudflare Web Analytics to count how often the app is opened and how long each session lasts. This data is anonymous and aggregated. Cloudflare explicitly does not use cookies, browser storage, IP addresses, or any other method to identify or track individual users. What we see is aggregate numbers only — for example, “200 sessions this week averaging 18 minutes” — with no way to identify who those sessions belong to.

The specific information Cloudflare's system records per visit is:

  • That a visit occurred, and its approximate duration
  • Which country the visit came from (derived from your IP address at the network level; the IP address itself is not stored or logged)
  • Browser type and device type (e.g. “Android / Chrome”), used only in aggregate

What we do not collect

  • No names, email addresses, or any information that could identify you
  • No account is required and none is created
  • No cookies are set, before or after you open the app
  • No persistent identifiers of any kind
  • No location beyond country level, and only in aggregate
  • Nothing typed or entered during gameplay

How data is used

The aggregate statistics are used solely to understand whether the app is being used and for how long — for example, to decide whether to continue developing it. The data is never sold, shared with advertisers, or used for any commercial purpose beyond this.

The analytics data is processed by Cloudflare on our behalf. Cloudflare's own privacy practices are described in their privacy policy. Cloudflare's Web Analytics product is specifically designed to be privacy-first and does not use visitor data for advertising.
